The Stupid Parable

Nopants the Monk pulled himself up the mountain. It was a painful hike, and by the time he reached the top, he was bloody, out of breath, and fatigued. He turned and faced the valley below, and addressed the village he had come from.

“You’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid, you’re stupid!” he preached.

His voice echoed all over creation. “You are dumb and stupid, and in addition dumb, but especially stupid.” No Pants panted, falling to his knees, out of breath.

Nopants felt a hand on his shoulder. Bung Fu the Fool was also on top of the mountain, apparently having taken the escalator.

“You’re right,” Bung Fu said to Nopants, “It’s so obvious from up here.”

Nopants glared at Bung Fu. “There’s an escalator??”

“Yep,” said Bung Fu as he rode it back down on his way to lunch. “See you around!”

Guided meditation

So what’s the point of guided meditation? I mean, isn’t it supposed to be a personal journey to enlightenment? Well, that’s the stated purpose at least.
The problem is that breaking out of a well established pattern of thought is hard. It’s too easy to get caught in a cycle of self reinforcement. That’s not enlightenment, that’s masturbation. Mind, masturbation can be enlightening and meditative, but that’s another sect’s business, or at least another post.
Think of guided meditation as mediating with training wheels. Most people need a little help escaping the routine and the permitted, at least at first. It’s helpful to get exposed to an outside perspective to get started on the whole pondering thing. Even if it’s cockamamy nonsense, it’s a crack in the established structure.
It’s a start.
That’s why we do these prompted thought experiments in the fractal cult. We’re helping each other break out and Think. We’re raising questions that we might not otherwise think to ask.
Like “Why do these prompt things?”
Best fishes,
Saint Amir Zetathustra, heretic.

On Writing Discordian Lit

We Discordians are all holy as fuck, and we have a direct line to Eris (dunno if she actually listens to these voice mails though).

That being said, there’s a lot of Discordian works that are essentially self-help books written by people who shouldn’t be giving advice to anybody.

There’s also a lot of hmmm, let’s say “low-effort” discordian lit,where somebody sat down and mashed the keyboard for 15 minutes, copy and pasted a bunch of kopyleft material they found, and said TA DAAA.

My advice to people adding to the Discordian library - think for yourself hard about it. Don’t just spout crazy sounding stuff in the spirit of Mal and Omar. Try to bring something new to the table. Write something that other people will find interesting, useful, challenging, whatever – but be original, take your time, and make a mark nobody’s made before.

Consciousness Exercise

So this is a fun exercise. Start from a single thought. Keep going, stream-of-consciousness style, until you figure out what Discordia means to you. Post the whole thought process in whatever medium you like.
My example is below the cut. 

Feed, breed, raise chaos. The Chaos of the new form is fed on the old forms; feed it well on interesting and worthwhile things and your chaos will be compelling. Feed it on dreck and drudgery and dullardry and the days of delusion slipping from the cradle to the chains of the slave, to an ill-earned outmode abode in the grave, and your chaos will still be chaos and it may appeal to those who need it more. So is it better to compel with beauty and complexity and art, or is it better to feed and breed and meet the need? Does commodity cheapen chaos? Does commodity cheapen art? What is value? What do you value? What do I value? I value originality. I value style - auteur. I value vision, and I like it very big or very small and almost nowhere in between. Telescopes or microscopes; my jelly and water camera is my own, and I don’t need yours. I like significance and beauty and life and death and love and hate, passion fulfilled or denied, honesty and intention or evil intention; if I want a character poisoned by its own stupidity and devoid of poetry, I’ll look around with jelly cameras and meat refractors and reflectors and by Eris I will see all of that I need. Reality is poetry, but art is poetry distilled. Taste the poetry in the everyday if you can. Art is life is poetry is music is the visual is the aural is the kinesthetic is everything and nothing and it is all chaos.
Taste the chaos. Develop a taste for chaos in others.
That is my purpose. That is what Discord means to me.
