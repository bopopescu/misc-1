I have begun attending meditation class, and one of the things I learned from the last class was the following:

I have been aware for a few years now that desires can be frustrating when we can’t sate them, and that we need to be able to let go of them when they are interfering with our lives (cf. Buddha). But what I was not aware of, is how often I do something because I subconsciously am trying to satisfy a desire, which I might be aware of in one context, but not in another (framing bias!). The problem here is you might be trying to achieve your goal in a wholly retarded way, but are simply not aware of it.

Hence my new mantra: WHY ARE YOU DOING THIS?

I am reading a wonderful book by Gould about the Structure of Evolutionary Theory. While the focus is on genetic evolution, I feel much of it can be applied in Sociocultural domains too.

The diagram here depicts how, for any given evolved trait process - we must consider three possible poles of explanation: I) Functional - that is, the trait is a Adaptation to current surroundings, II) Formal - the trait is due to Constraints of Structure. III) History - the trait is explained by Historical Contingency.

Mostly - things are some combination of all three. For example - if we ask, why is the space bar on the keyboard so wide and particularly placed? Clearly its Functionally adapted to be hit by thumbs since its often used. But why are all the letters on the keyboard placed where they are? The answer is Historical and Formal - back when typewriters were used there was a Formal restriction preventing frequently used key combinations from being placed close to one another because of jamming. But as this Formal restriction disappeared, people were used to the old configuration, so it remained.
