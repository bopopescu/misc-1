by Cramulus

They took me to the edge of the valley, where the ground drops off. I was leading them to my monument, I’m not sure which one.

The valley is full of monuments, every single one that has ever existed, all crowded against one another like a subway car at rush hour. The Eiffel tower, the Lincoln Memorial, the statue of Einstein —- (its plaque:

The right to search for truth implies also a duty; one must not conceal any part of what one has recognized to be true. 

         ) —- and somewhere in that monumental mess, my monument sits in this dream valley.

As I climb down the slope, I see it and instantly recognize it.

The Pantheon.  

          MY Pantheon.

The Romans worshiped so many dead gods there, but now it’s mine.

I turn around and I say “home”

But I turned around too early, the dream fades

     like Eurydice
