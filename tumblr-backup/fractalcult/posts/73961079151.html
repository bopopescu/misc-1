Question regarding the Fourth Commandment

Yuki Euphoric, Eternal Pope of the Fnords, asks:

Can I substitute my bunless hotdog for an offler sausage on fridays? It’s a tough question for our time, but even as a devout discordian, it is hard to walk the line between blasphemy and cultural acceptance here in ankh morpork. Can a well learned, scrupulous episkopos help me?

His Hornswoggliness Ixxie Proxyweaver KSC responds:

This is a matter of righteous dispute amongst the various sects of Discordianism how to interpret the fourth commandment.

The Orthodoxy - being consistently in early illumination - follows the third commandment to the letter, and partakes with joy of hot dog buns. The predominant Heterodoxy on the other takes the fifth commandment literally and disbelieves the fourth commandment, but eventually also disbelieves the fifth commandment and become the Paradoxy. The Paradoxy then proceeds to lament whether the fourth commandment forbids the Sausage, the Buns or Both, and opts instead to reject Paradoxy in favour of the Proxydoxy. Now, since the Proxydoxy approximately mocks all doxies, it knocks its own oxymoronic moxie and opts in place Metadoxy. The Metadoxy consults its pineal gland and doesn’t have any idea what an offler sausage is.
